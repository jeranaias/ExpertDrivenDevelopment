Course Catalog

Five courses cover every role, from first-time AI users to experienced builders to supervisors evaluating proposals. Start with the required AI Fluency Fundamentals, then progress through hands-on building courses at your own pace.

Required

Course 1: AI Fluency Fundamentals

The universal prerequisite. Teaches the six applied judgment skills that separate sustained AI adopters from the 80% who quit. No tool-building. No coding. Just the management skills that research shows predict AI adoption.

Duration
2 hours
Audience
All personnel
Outcome
Understand the six 2011 skills, recognize the jagged frontier, know when to trust AI output
View Course
Elective

Course 2: Builder Orientation

For people who want to go beyond daily AI use and build tools. Hands-on building from start to finish. Live builds, debugging, and problem decomposition.

Duration
2 hours
Audience
Aspiring builders
Prerequisite
AI Fluency Fundamentals
Outcome
Build a working prototype, apply task decomposition and iterative refinement
View Course
Elective

Course 3: Platform Training

Build three complete tools on Power Platform practicing both centaur and cyborg work patterns. This is where students become capable of producing deployable tools.

Duration
4 hours
Audience
Builders
Prerequisite
Builder Orientation
Outcome
Build 3 complete tools using centaur and cyborg work patterns
View Course
Elective

Course 4: Advanced Workshop

Move from individual to organizational capability. Frontier mapping, complex builds, verification protocols, group debugging, and teaching methodology.

Duration
4 hours
Audience
Experienced builders
Prerequisite
At least one deployed tool
Outcome
Map the frontier, build verification protocols, teach others

Recommended

Course 5: Supervisor Orientation

The highest-leverage 30 minutes in the program. Learn to create permission culture, evaluate AI-assisted output, and preserve junior development.

Duration
30 minutes
Audience
Leadership
Prerequisite
None
Outcome
Create permission culture, evaluate proposals, understand apprentice problem
